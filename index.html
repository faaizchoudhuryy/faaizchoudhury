About Muazzaz Ahmed Choudhury

Muazzaz Ahmed Choudhury, also known as Faaiz Choudhury, is a dedicated B.A. LL.B. student from the culturally rich town of Karimganj, Assam. Known for his quiet determination and deep-rooted sense of purpose, he strives to grow through knowledge, expression, and service.

His academic journey is defined by a passion for law, eloquence, and intellectual clarity. Whether through writing, legal studies, or artistic expression, Muazzaz engages in pursuits that refine his reasoning and amplify his voice. He is also an active writer, having authored numerous thought-provoking articles on Medium, Youth Ki Awaaz, and LinkedIn, where he addresses issues such as social justice, politics, education reforms, and cultural identity.

Belonging to a family with a distinguished lineage that traces back to Mesopotamia and Mughal India, Muazzaz draws inspiration from his ancestral legacy. His forefather, Muhammad Karim Baksh, a noble from Mesopotamia, arrived in Delhi in 1599 AD during Emperor Akbar’s reign to become a disciple of the revered Sufi saint Hazrat Baqi Billah (R.A.). His son, Nawab Jan Muhammad Khan Bahadur, was appointed Faujdar of Bihar in 1663 and later Faujdar of Sylhet Sarkar in 1666 AD under Emperor Aurangzeb. The family’s history further shines through Dewan Arman Ali Choudhury, the zamindar of Ujandihi, Karimganj.

During the Partition of 1947, while most Choudhury families of Ujandihi migrated to East Pakistan (now Bangladesh), his great-grandfather, Dr. Rosman Ali Choudhury—a British-era physician and freedom fighter—chose to remain in India. His belief in democracy, secularism, and national integrity continues to inspire Muazzaz’s aspirations.

Through the written word, legal advocacy, and creative endeavors, Muazzaz Ahmed Choudhury seeks to honor this legacy by contributing meaningfully to his generation’s discourse—rooted in clarity, culture, and conviction.
